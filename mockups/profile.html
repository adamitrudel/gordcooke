BIOGRAPHY

Welcome to my website that is dedicated to helping the building industry and trade contractors build, renovate and sell more efficient, comfortable, healthier and more durable homes. As a professional engineer, trainer, author and industry consultant for the last 25 years, I have had the pleasure of working with great building professionals across North America who are dedicated to a path of continual improvement.

As in all the training I do, I welcome your comments, feedback and interaction. We are a large and diverse industry – manufacturers, designers, consultants, suppliers, trade contractors, sales agents, building officials, energy raters, government representatives, builders and renovators. All of us have an important viewpoint on the industry, so please engage in the betterment of our industry by using and responding to the information presented.

Thank you: Gord Cooke

Biography

For the past two decades, Gord Cooke has been an effective and passionate educator and advocate for better building practices, improved indoor air quality and energy efficiency. He has a unique talent of taking the complex issues of building science and breaking them into easily understood elements with practical real-world applications for all audiences. Gord’s building science training curricula covers the gamut from creating and delivering basic and advanced building science principles through managing indoor air quality to HVAC system design to an intensive sales training workshop. He has pioneered new training programs designed to assist builders in the sales and marketing of higher performing, energy efficient homes -- helping builders more effectively tell their story to customers, increase their margins and sell more homes.

Gord has delivered sessions at such conferences as the NAHB’s International Builders Show, Energy Design Conference, JLC Live, West Coast Green and the National Green Building Conference and is an active Energy & Environmental Building Association (EEBA) board member and certified trainer. He authored the Build Comfort Program and provided instruction for both the Union and Enbridge Gas companies. Additionally, Gord has authored and co-authored numerous white papers and research studies related to indoor air quality and home performance issues, contributes a monthly IAQ column to HPAC magazine and is a certified R-2000 Design Evaluator and Trainer.

MISSION & VALUES

Gord’s mission is to help improve the quality, comfort, durability and energy efficiency of new homes in Ontario and North America through education and innovative products.

Goals

To provide technical representation of innovative energy efficient products to the building industry.
To provide products for the improvement of indoor air quality.
To provide services and support to builders to help improve building practices and meet and exceed building program guidelines.
To provide technical and sales & marketing training to the housing market and related building industries to lessen the impact of building on the environment.

Values

Gord VALUES :

  • The outdoor and indoor living environments. All of the products and services he provides are aimed at lessening the environmental footprint of building houses without compromising health or comfort.
  • His business relationships. Gord endeavours to build long term relationships with his clients, based on honesty, trust, good service and mutual respect.
  • His staff and associates. Gord strives to strengthen their individual understanding and knowledge base by encouraging professional development, and by providing strong leadership and mentoring to maintain the integrity of his company.
  • His reputation. Gord takes pride in being a leader in new building technologies, being easy to work with and for, and in being a first call resource for his clients.

EXPERTISE & EXPERIENCE

Gord has a wide range of expertise and experience that allows him to appreciate and respond to a wide range of clients and their issues :

Technical Expertise

  • His formal background in the industry started with ventilation and HVAC systems in both residential and institutional buildings. That led him to the study of indoor air quality and he was the first person to complete the Canada Mortgage and Housing Indoor Air Quality Investigators program. He conducts audits in existing homes for homeowners with serious indoor air quality issues.
  • He now trains a variety of HVAC contractors, home inspectors and health officials on how to investigate complex IAQ issues in existing homes in a practical way.
  • He has been conducting energy audits and performance testing new homes under the R-2000 and ENERGY STAR for New Homes program for over 20 years, working with large volume and custom builders to identify the most practical solutions for improving energy efficiency and durability.
  • As a young engineer, he helped design innovative ventilation products for schools and homes and more recently has helped government agencies and manufacturers evaluate new innovative technologies for the building industry – including HVAC systems, solar, water heating options and insulation products.
  • Gord is an active Building Canada team member, helping large volume builders find cost effective solutions to improving energy efficiency

Sales Experience :

  • Gord has run a successful sales agency since 1986, always concentrating on developing and growing new markets for innovative building industry products.
  • He relates this valuable experience when training HVAC contractors, window sales agents and especially new home sales agents on the essential sales process elements for high performance new homes and products.

Program Development :

  • Gord became the interim manager of the Ontario R-2000 program, Canada’s leading housing energy efficiency program, in the early 1990’s. His experience with leading builders as their energy evaluator helped the program focus on the key elements of a new home energy efficiency program.
  • Gord was also the co-author of a college diploma course on building science. He worked with the college to develop both an in-house program for young students and an out-reach program for industry clients.
  • Since that time, he has consulted with utilities, government agencies and Standards writing committees to help them implement new programs and standards to the building industry.

This wide circle of experience allows Gord to appreciate the needs of the widest possible range of clients in the building industry – from manufacturers to homeowners, building officials to trade contractors, utilities to educational institutions. It is Gord’s unique ability to take complex issues and view them from many focal points, always with an eye to finding practical and cost effective solutions, that is of such great value to clients.
